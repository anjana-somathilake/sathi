About

Seelawathi Sati Sewana

Seelawathi Sati Sewana is a monastery located in the city of Kaduwela -Greater Colombo, Sri Lanka, surrounded by the lush greenery and overlooking the majestic river of Kelani. This valuable land of the monastery has been a donation to the ownership of “Nissarana Vanaya” by late Mrs Seelawathi Hettiarachchi in the year 2016.

The quiet setting and thick foliage of the area brings in the perfect mind set for practicing yogis at the monastery. Still in its inception, Seelawathi Sati Sewana has conducted retreats for adults constantly since the inaugural meditation retreat of the monastery, which was held in April,2017, by Chief preceptor and Abbot of Nissarana Vanaya Most Ven. Uda Eriyagama Dhammajiva Maha Thero. The monastery is being activated and overseen as a branch of Mitirigala Nissarana Vanaya. Both a specialist and exponent of the Dhamma,Venerable Dhamma Vihari Thero stands as the existent Chief Preceptor of the monastery. He is a scholar of the distinguished meditation master Most Venerable Uda Eriyagama Dhammajīva Mahā Thero of Nissarana Vanaya. Venerable Dhamma Vihari Thero has profound knowledge and practice of both the Samatha and Vipassanā meditation methods.

Sati Pasala

The main emphasis of the monastery is to facilitate programs to introduce & cultivate Sati – mindfulness for Children. At present the Sati Pasala of Seelawathi Sati Sewana Kaduwela is well over one year in its training of young minds and involves a group of dedicated young voluntary teachers who themselves are practitioners. The object of the Sati Pasala at Seelawathi Sati Sewana is to bring about the regular practice and awareness of “sati” amongst children for the well -being of self and association with the society at large. The first ever sati camp for children was held alongside the inaugural adults’ retreat in April ,2017. At Sati Pasala, children from ages as young as 3 up to the age 16 show great enthusiasm in learning & practicing activities related to “sati” The monastery is being further developed to accommodate a greater number of children in a single program and will be the established centre for Sati Pasala activities in the years to come.
